About Kryptos.

Kryptos is a sculpture built at the CIA headquarters in Langley, Virginia. The sculpture is divided into 4 sections. Over the course of almost thirty years, the first 3 sections have been solved. There is one section left.

Learn about Kryptos & It's Solvers

The solvers of this code have been very secretive of their achievement. Why?

The first person to announce to the public that they have solved some of the legendary code was James Gillogly in 1999. His announcement was followed with the announcement from the CIA that said that one of their employees named David Stein had already solved the first three parts of the code in 1998.

The NSA had then claimed that some of there employees had already solved the first three passages in 1993. In 2013, the NSA released the documents confirming the claims. The documents proved that a small team of NSA cryptanalysts led by Ken Miller, along with Dennis McDaniels, did indeed solve most of Kryptos in 1993.
